 We lost $2,000 on this deep cleaning job and here's the story.  A few weeks ago we got called to clean an elderly woman's home because of her age,  she physically couldn't clean anymore and needed our help.  At first glance the house just looked dusty but once we started cleaning,  we saw it smoke everywhere all over the walls.  On the windows inside the bathroom fan, this wasn't surfacer.  This was the years of smoke buildup.  We started with the walls, one pass, still brown, two passes, still stained.  That's when we looked at each other and realized we completely underestimated this job.  The quote we gave was way too low because smoke doesn't wipe off in one pass.  It took three full passes with diluted degreaser just to break it down.  Then we opened the bathroom fan, thick black residue packed inside.  Every crevice had to be scrubbed by hand.  In the kitchen, the cabinets looked beige, until we wiped them and the cloth turned yellow instantly.  Windows had a sticky film, light switches were coated in smoke.  What we thought was a six hour clean turn into a full day with multiple people  and with every extra hour, we knew we were losing money.  But when we finished, the walls were bright again, the air fell lighter.  And when the client saw the final results, she got so emotional she started crying.  She kept looking around like she couldn't believe it was the same house.  And in that moment, even though we had lost two thousand dollars,  seeing her joy made every second worth it.
