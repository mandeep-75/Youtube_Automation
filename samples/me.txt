 If you have an older PC or Mac, listen up.  Minecraft Java is finally replacing its ancient engine  with something that actually works.  For years, Java has run on OpenGL,  an API for the 90s that's a nightmare for slow laptops  and integrated graphics to render.  It's why your game stutters every single frame.  Most of us rely on mods like fabulously optimized  or sodium, just to make the game playable,  but moaching just announced they are officially  switching the entire game to Vulcan.  If OpenGL is like a wooden pickaxe,  Vulcan is like a diamond pickaxe,  it slashes CPU overhead,  which is the number one reason why laptop struggled  with chunk loading and entity lag.  This is the first step towards the vibrant visuals update,  giving low-end players better lighting  and performance without needing a $2,000 GPU.
